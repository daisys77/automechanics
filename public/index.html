    How To Check Your Oil

    It is very easy to check your oil

    Do not wait for the oil light to come on


    Step 1

    Ensure that your car is position on a flat road, turn on your engine so that the tempature becomes slightly warm, after that turn the engine to ensure the level of the oil has settled.

    Step 2

    The next step is to pull up the bonnet and then the dipstick.

    Step 3

    Clean the dipstick properly and then replace it

    Step 4

     The next step is to remove the dipstick once again and properly read the oil level. The level initially should be between the minimum and maximum line.

    Step 5

    To tell if your oil needs topping up is to see if the level of the oil is below or close to the minimum marker

    How can i tell if my clutch is going


    The clutch is one of the most important parts of any vehicle. If there is no clutch then there would be no chance for you to change between gears. Just like various other components for cars, a clutch can wear out for a long period of time.

     

    What exactly does the clutch do?

    The clutch allows you to separate the wheels from the engine which allows you to change between the gears and while the engine is still running it can come to a stop. There are 3 crucial parts of a clutch and there are; the flywheel, clutch plate and pressure plate.

     

    How long does the clutch last until?

    Usually, the clutch is supposed to last between 50,000 miles to 100,000 miles. It is designed to last for that period however, there are many factors that impact how long your clutch can really last. Some of the factors could be how you drive the car (the style), the quality of your car and how well you maintain your car. An example of how your clutch can be affected is if you constantly hit stop and start while driving, this could wear out quicker as you will be changing between the lower gears often.

    What you should be looking out for

    You will most likely need a clutch replacement if the following applies to you:

    • Can turn the engine but acceleration is very poor
    • Finding it difficult when changing the gears
    • Vibrating, sticking, spongy or a loose clutch pedals when it is pressed
    • An unusual sound when it is pressed.

    Do I need a new exhaust?


    How does the exhaust system usually work?

    The exhaust has many components which include sensors, catalytic converter, manifold, exhaust pipe and silencer. All of these components must work fully and should be in good condition in order for the system to work successfully.

    The manifold gathers injurious gases that may be produced by the engine's cylinders and then it funnels it into the catalytic converter as long you are driving. The converter’s role is to remove any injurious elements such as hydrogen monoxide and carbon monoxide from the gas. Furthermore, the noise level is reduced as the gas goes through the silencer.

     

    For how long will the exhaust last?

    As similar to other components it really depends on how you use it and there are several factors that could affect the lifespan of the exhaust such as the usage, the way in which you drive and the model of the car.

    Modern car exhaust is designed so that it lasts until the car does and it is usually made of stainless steel or chrome. Non-stainless steels usually would last you for a solid 5 years depending on if it’s high quality.

    How to tell if you need a new exhaust?

    Here are some of the signs you need to look out for:

    • Rust: inspect to see whether it is splitting or if there any signs of rust. In some cases, it is common for older cars, however, if there is extreme damage done then it is a sign that you would need a replacement.
    • Engine sound: Have you noticed that the sound of your engine has become louder at all? If there is a minor damage then that could cause a major difference in how the sound of the engine is. If you notice that the car is making more of a rattling noise rather than a rumbling noise then this could mean that the catalytic converter needs repairing or that it is loose. You need to know that one of the most fragile parts is the catalytic converter in the exhaust system. Also, if you are driving on a road bump or even on bumpy roads, it could damage it easily without a doubt.

    How can I tell if my turbo is blown?


    A turbo is intended to boost engine power and it a smart device. Nowadays most car manufacturers use turbo for greater power for cars. It is likely that turbos do become damage during a long period of time even though it is designed to last for a very long time.

    How does the turbo work?

    To produce power there is a combination of air and fuel. In order for you to increase the power of your engine as well as ensuring the cost is low is to add more air to it. The air pump forces additional air into the engine's cylinders which provides you with considerably greater horsepower.

    How can I tell if my turbo is blown?

    Here are some of the signs:

     

    • Power loss
    • Smoke coming from the exhaust which is blue or grey
    • The dashboard of the engine’s light shows
    • The acceleration is loud or slower
    • Having a lot of problems with maintaining high speeds

     

    Make sure you check for this things. This could really help you figure out the problem

     

    What are the causes of a damaged turbo?

    There are many reasons why it can be caused

    • The cars model/age: It is known that most turbos are designed so that it could last you for a very long time however, it is not always the case as there can have multiple reasons. The quality of the fitted turbo could be one of the reasons or how hard you usually drive your car.
    • Oil of the engine: It is important that the turbo has oil in order for it to work properly. If the oil is wrong or the quality of oil is bad it can affect the car as the carbon deposits and contaminants will build up in the engine which will cause a lot of damage to your turbo.
    • Worn seals or cracks: if the compressors have damaged seals in-between then the engine will most likely have an oil leak into the system so, therefore, the turbo will have difficulty in pushing out air pressure.

     

    What should I do with the blown turbo?

    If you believe you have a blown turbo then it is always a good idea to get it checked out as soon as possible. It is also important that you get it checked out by a profession qualified technician, this would also help you out as you could get more help with your new replacement.

    How do I know when my breaks need replacing


    It is important to know that brakes are one of the most important parts for all vehicles. You must ensure that when it is needed to be replaced, it should be done no doubt as soon as possible.

    Usually, around 50,000 miles is when the replacement of the brakes are needed. However, it is not always for everyone as for how long the brakes last has many factors such as the way you drive, the car type you have and the pads that were fitted previously in your car will affect the lifespan of your car brakes.

    It is vital that if you are unsure of the brakes or if there are any problems with them then you should talk to a technician about it straight away and have them check it out. Do make sure that the technician you are getting help from is qualified for the job.

    Here are some of the signs you need to look out for of worn brakes:

    • Leaks on the wheel: if there are any hoses spilt in the system or the connection is loose then fluid and air will easily leak out which would affect your ability to brake. Make sure you look for any fluid around the inner wheel which is usually clear but does have a slight smell.

     

    • Pull left and right: whenever you brake, does it allow you to pull left and right? The pads need to be equally applied once you press the brake pedal and if one is worn than the other one then it will affect you as pulling to a certain side could occur. Also, it could be the failure of the brake calliper.

     

    • Mileage: Brakes and pads need to be replaced in pairs and also at the same time. Brakes do get worse as time goes naturally which is important to know. Listen carefully to following as this as this could help you out tremendously. Pads need to be replaced every 30,000 miles and discs at 50,000 miles.

     

    • Unusual sounds: if you hear sounds that do sound unusual when the brake pedal is pressed then this could give you a sign that they could be an issue with your system. There may be a weird squeaking sound which is usually heard when it is raining or parked in water for a long time. The discs could build up rust, however, do not panic as it will wear off once you are driving again.

     

    • Vibration: It is important to get your brakes checked out if your brake pedal or your steering wheel vibrates when you are slowing down. This could mean that your brakes disc is damaged.

    How to identify common suspension issue


    Car suspension allows you to have an amazing relaxing journey. When driving on a road surface that has lumps and bumps, the car suspension absorbs it to allow you to accelerate and brake safely without creating a danger towards other road users. 

    Over a long period of time, you realise that the car’s suspension is starting to get worn out and can feel that journeys are getting tougher than they once were. Your vehicle has a worn out suspension system can have a massive impact on your stopping distance around 20%, so it is vital that you get these problems situated immediately.

    Realising vehicle suspension problems can be difficult; however, here are some aspects you need to look out for:

    The overall stability of the vehicle

    If a spring within the vehicle system has been disengaged or damaged, the vehicle has a possibility that it will be lower one side than the other. 

    There is a method called a bounce test. The first step you would have to undertake is to park your vehicle on a level surface. The weight has to be kept on the front or back of your vehicle when doing this record how many times the vehicle bounces throughout this process before it ends. If the vehicle bounces more than two or three times, there is a possibility that there is a fault within the vehicle and will have to be checked immediately.

    Tyres Depth

    A usual tyre tread might be a sign that there is something wrong and must be checked.  A poorly suspension system can be noticed if your tyres are majorly worn than the others on the inside and outside.

    Handling it poorly

    There are many factors that could affect the way your cars being dealt with. For example, if your wheel alignment is incorrect or the response of the steering is poor then it will make it very difficult for you to control. If you realise a knocking sound when pulling or turning corners then it could be a sign of a failing suspension system. This is because it has been damaged so the cars stable is not allowing you to turn well and could be very dangerous for you. Remember safety is important and ensure you are beware of the risks.

    A bumpy car ride

    If the car ride feels bumpier than it is usually then it could be that there is a problem with the suspension. Most like other vehicle components, a lot of them wore out over the time and must be a replacement.
